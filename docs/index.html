Your voice never leaves your Mac.
Not “encrypted in transit.” Not “anonymized.” Not sent. Audio goes from your microphone into a model running on your machine, and is discarded the moment text exists. The only required network call is the one-time model download. Cloud text cleanup happens only if you add your own API key — and it only ever sees text, never audio.
Don't take our word for it — read the source. That's the point of open source.

Questions

Really free? What's the catch?

MIT-licensed, community-built, no account, no telemetry. Whisper is free to run; your Mac provides the compute. There is nothing to sell you.

How accurate is it?

Whisper runs on-device with your pick of models from tiny to large — trade speed for accuracy per your Mac. Your personal dictionary fixes names and jargon once, forever, and the Know-Me interview teaches cleanup your tone.

Does AI cleanup send my text to the cloud?

Only if you add your own API key for a cloud provider — the default cloud path is OpenRouter Gemma 4, and keys are stored only on your Mac. Choose local Ollama, or turn cleanup off entirely, for a fully on-device flow. Audio never goes anywhere, regardless.

Which Macs?

macOS 12 Monterey or newer. Apple Silicon flies; Intel Macs work with smaller models. Setup is honest with you about what your hardware can handle.

Where does my data live?

In ~/.openvoiceflow on your Mac — transcripts, dictionary, profile, keys. Delete that folder and every trace is gone. Nothing is synced anywhere.

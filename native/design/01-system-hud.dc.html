01 · THE VOICELINE
One continuous line. Never bars.
Silence breathes. ±1 px ripple on a ~4 s period. The line is alive even when you aren't speaking — it never reads as dead or frozen.
Speech is smooth. Three blended harmonics driven by RMS amplitude, 80 ms smoothing. Organic, calligraphic — explicitly not an EQ.
Ember means live. The line turns ember only while voice is detected — a truthful mic indicator, in the same family as macOS's own orange dot.
DO · round caps, 2 pt DO · one line per surface DO · stay inside the container DON'T · EQ bars DON'T · gradients as identity DON'T · stacked or mirrored copies

04 · ACCESSIBILITY — DESIGNED, NOT DEGRADED
Reduce Motion
Waveform → 9-dot level meter, updated ≤ 2 Hz. Coil → three dots pulsing opacity at 1 Hz. Shimmer → caption only. All translate/scale transitions → 130 ms crossfades. Error shake → border flash. Nothing is merely turned off; each state has an equivalent.
Reduce Transparency
Capsule blur → opaque surface, border strengthened +4 % alpha, shadow kept. Contrast of every label re-verified ≥ 4.5:1 against the solid fill.
Increased Contrast
Line 2 → 2.5 pt; secondary ink promotes one step; hairlines double. Ember swaps to its text-safe shade (4.6:1) everywhere it labels.
VoiceOver
The HUD is an accessibility element; every state change posts a polite announcement: "Dictation on — listening" · "Transcribing" · "Cleaning up" · "Inserted 13 words" · "30 seconds left" · errors verbatim. The hotkey rehearsal in onboarding is fully narrated.

07 · WHY BOTTOM-CENTER + ENGINEERING NOTES
Bottom-center — chosen
A stable home you can read from peripheral vision. Never occludes the caret in practice (text lives mid-to-upper screen). Position parity with Wispr Flow makes switching frictionless — we beat them on behavior: truly non-activating, a truthful live-voice line, designed silence, and errors that recover in one line.
Near-cursor — rejected
Caret rects are unreliable across apps (AX gaps in Electron, browsers, terminals). It jitters as you dictate, and it covers the very words that are about to arrive.
Notch-adjacent — rejected
Collides with the system mic indicator and menu-bar extras, disappears in full-screen, and sits where your eyes aren't. Kept as a hidden preference candidate, not the default.
NSPanel · nonactivatingPanel + .hudWindow · level .statusBar · canJoinAllSpaces + fullScreenAuxiliary
focus — never key; error buttons use click-through hit-testing only over their own bounds
material — NSVisualEffectView .hudWindow; reduceTransparency → solid surface token
wave — 43 RMS buckets @ 60 Hz from AVAudioEngine → 80 ms EMA → CADisplayLink canvas/Metal layer
displays — HUD appears on the screen owning keyboard focus; re-homes between takes, never mid-take
announcements — NSAccessibility.post(.announcementRequested) per state, polite priority

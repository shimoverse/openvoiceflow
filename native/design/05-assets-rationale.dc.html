E · THE ONE-PAGER — WHY THIS WINS
Five decisions
1 · One motif, five surfaces. Icon, menu bar, HUD, hero, cards — the same continuous line. Memory compounds; Wispr owns a pill, we own a line.
2 · Privacy you can see. Containment is the motion language: the voice coils into the Mac and lands at the caret. The claim and the animation are the same sentence.
3 · Parity where it's free, superiority where it counts. Bottom-center matches Wispr muscle memory; silence, errors, long takes and a11y are designed states they don't have.
4 · Native to the bone. Template icons, pure SF, NSPanel that never steals focus, springs tuned like AppKit. Feels like Apple shipped it — remembered because nobody else did.
5 · Trust as interface. Checksums up front, Keychain badges, permission priming, a narrated model download. Every scary moment redesigned into a promise kept.
Where it beats Wispr Flow
Price — $0 forever vs $144/yr. The comparison table does the selling.
Trust — their audio goes to their cloud; ours can't leave. Open source makes the claim checkable.
Resilience — airplane mode, outages, NDAs, hospitals: we keep working.
Craft — designed silence, one-line error recovery, Reduce Motion equivalents, VoiceOver announcements. Award criteria, not checkbox a11y.
Dignity — no account, no telemetry, no upsell. The app never asks for anything back.
